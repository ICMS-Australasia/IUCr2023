IUCr 2023 Abstracts Now Open

The IUCr 2023 countdown begins. A major milestone for the IUCr 2023 Congress – one year to the start of the 26th Congress and General Assembly of the International Union of Crystallography. Our Call for Abstracts and Bursary applications are all open today allowing three months (until 21 November) to submit. Registration is also now open, early bird savings are on offer until 15 February 2023.

It was our privilege to attend ACA 2022 - American Crystallographic Association 72nd Annual Meeting in Portland, Oregon. This first ACA face-to-face meeting since the pandemic. The excitement and appetite for the IUCr Congress in Australia was palpable. We met over 500 crystallography friends, many of whom were first-time attendees and who were grateful for the opportunity to reunite.

Later this month the team will be in Versailles at the 33rd European Crystallographic Meeting between 23- 27 of August 2022. Our LOC member Helen Maynard Casey will be presenting and there will be a few of the Melbournians in attendance to cheer her on. Stop by our stand at #29 for your touch of Australia.

Kind regards,

Michael Parker
Chair, IUCr 2023

Call for Abstracts Now Open until 21 November 2022

We invite you to submit your abstract to secure your role in ensuring IUCr 2023 is the place to be for all researchers and industry partners of Crystallography in 2023. The scientific work of the IUCr is conducted primarily through its Commissions. There are 22 individual Commissions amd Committees that comprise groups of scientists representing the key areas of crystallographic sciences including:

Aperiodic Crystals Magnetic Structures
Biological Macromolecules Mathematical and Theoretical Crystallography
Crystal Growth and Characterization of Materials Neutron Scattering
Crystallographic Computing NMR Crystallography and Related Methods
Crystallographic Teaching Powder Diffraction
Crystallography in Art and Cultural Heritage Quantum Crystallography
Crystallography of Materials Small-Angle Scattering
Diffraction Microstructure Imaging Structural Chemistry
Electron Crystallography Synchrotron and XFEL Radiation
High Pressure XAFS
Inorganic and Mineral Structures

Thus ensuring the IUCr 2023 Scientific Program will be as extensive as it will be interesting. The Program will be bolstered with a variety of Workshops, Panels and Special Events. If it is about crystallography and related fields in the structural sciences, it will be at IUCr 2023!

Daily prize draw winner – past IUCr President Marv Hackert

"Rose City"; "Stumptown"; "PDX" – Portland what a great place to reunite

Thank you to the ACA (American Crystallography Association) for hosting us in Portland. What a fantastic city and what a terrific event.

Our attendance not only raised awareness of the Congress, as many ACA were first time attendees, but it solidified our aim that IUCr 2023 in Melbourne aims to be a predominantly in-person event. Our conversations with delegates centred on the opportunities for potential delegates to attend both the business reason for travel and new life experiences. Like ACA – IUCr is a family friendly meeting. We intend to provide a safe environment for families to meet and fun zone for children to participate.

Our clip-on Koalas proved to be one of the most popular give-aways, as did our gigantic Koala photo backdrop! In total, we dispersed 700+ koalas – clearly we had more than one visit per delegate! Our bespoke sustainable Melbourne bamboo coffee cups were a very popular, as waste reduction is an important delegate value. It was encouraging to spot them in the exhibition instantly filled with hot or cold beverages.

Next week you can collect yours from our team in Versailles at the 33rd European Crystallographic Meeting between 23-27 of August 2022. See you there!

Introducing Bragg Your Pattern

Our wonderful community of Australian scientists from IUCr 2023 have created Bragg Your Pattern to provide fun and free STEM experiences to the general public.

Bragg Your Pattern will be offering a kaleidoscope of activities relating to patterns and crystals for everyone to enjoy, especially budding junior crystallographers.

The Bragg Your Pattern website is full of additional information. We want kids to be inspired by patterns around them and submit their pattern artwork in our Online Pattern Competition for the chance to win an awesome prize pack and have their artwork featured at IUCr 2023. Full teacher resources and student activity sheet are ready to download now for free
