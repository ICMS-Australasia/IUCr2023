However you say it - most of us will agree chocolate is a universal treat, that is justly celebrated on 7 July.

Chocolate is a very sophisticated material, and requires the right blend of sugar, milk and cocoa butter fats for it to taste right. An added complication is that you can get different crystal structures of cocoa butter depending on how you solidify it. That’s where crystallography has come in, to find the right conditions for the tastier packing of cocoa butter fat molecules!

There are just seven weeks to the start of the 26th Congress and General Assembly of the International Union of Crystallography. We promise an inspiring and innovative program showcasing keynote speakers and plenary speakers from across the world; with over award lectures, 700 specialised oral sessions and posters to be delivered, IUCr 2023 is a truly global representation of crystallography expertise. The Congress's social functions complement the scientific program to facilitate your networking needs and your culinary tastebuds! All of this knowledge and access to industry under one roof in Melbourne, Australia. It will be a magnificent event.

We urge you to join over 1,700+ crystallographers representing 72 countries, who will contribute to the strengthening of the global crystallography community. We promise you it will be worth the journey.

Happy World Chocolate Day,
